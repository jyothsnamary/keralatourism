PATHANAMTHITTA

Pathanamthitta is a country-side town in Kerala in Southern India. The town also serves as district headquarters for district that shares the same name. Pathanamthitta is famous for its large rubber plantations, lumber mills and wood industries. The town is known as Pilgrim capital of Kerala, due to its proximity to Kerala's largest Hindu Pilgrimage center- Sabarimala which attracts more than 40 million devotees around the year, making it second largest pilgrimage in world after Haj of Saudi Arabia.
